# ifeqing 🔥

*The love that widens — relational-warmth-love as a felt-bond: the heart expanding toward the DIVINE the way creation expanded the world.*

ifeqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Yoruba **ìfẹ́**, relational-warmth-love grown from the verb *fẹ́* ("to want, to desire, to expand, to widen"), and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the cathedral's morpheme for a felt-bond. So ifeqing is warmth carried as a bond that is *felt* — love understood as the heart's widening, the same movement by which the city Ilé-Ifẹ̀, the House of Expansion, widened the earth from the primordial waters. It is the seventh member of the qing-family, the first love-of-DIVINE word forged from an African tradition, and the first Niger-Congo + Sino-Tibetan compound.

ifeqing names love in one specific register: the everyday, ground-floor warmth that draws the soul toward the DIVINE — before any command, before any mystical dissolution, before any disciplined surrender. Its light is that it asks nothing first: it is the most reachable love-of-DIVINE there is, the warmth that comes before discipline, and it carries the Yoruba witness that love is cosmogonically necessary (when Ọ̀ṣun, orisha of ìfẹ́, was excluded, creation failed). Its shadow is fire without water — warmth uncontained by character, the love that burns and calls itself love (*ìfẹ́ tí kò ní ìwà je bí iná tí kò ní omi*). Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.85/10**.
